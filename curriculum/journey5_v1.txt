Journey 5 Overview
Source text: selected passages from Joshua, Judges, 1 and 2 Samuel, and 1 and 2 Kings. Journey 5 covers the arc from the crossing of the Jordan through the fall of the kingdoms. The pedagogical focus is on narrative consolidation: the child reads extended narrative passages fluently and encounters the full range of verb forms in active prose. The covenant vocabulary anchored in Journey 3 continues to appear with high frequency. The king-vocabulary cluster (melech, mamlacha, kisse, berit) is established as preparation for Journey 6's Mosiah focus.
Journey 5 is 60 lessons across seven units: the crossing of the Jordan, judges and deliverers, the rise of Samuel, David's anointing and psalms, the building of Solomon's temple, the divided kingdom, and the prophetic voices of Elijah and Elisha.
Pedagogical Note
By Journey 5 the child is reading pointed and unpointed Hebrew with roughly equal facility. Niqqud fading has now advanced to the point that about 60 percent of text the child sees is unpointed. New vocabulary and proper names continue to receive full pointing. The child now reads narrative passages of 10 to 15 verses as a single unit rather than verse-by-verse.
 
Unit 1. Crossing the Jordan (Lessons 1 to 8)
Source text: Joshua 1 through 6 (selected verses). The commission of Joshua, the spies at Jericho, the crossing of the Jordan, the fall of Jericho. The Exodus pattern repeats (water parts, enemies fall) and the child recognizes the echo.
Lesson 1. Be Strong and of Good Courage
Verse: Joshua 1:6
חֲזַק וֶאֱמָץ
chazak ve-ematz
Gloss: Be strong and of good courage.
Teaching focus: Short, weighty covenant-commission verse. Reinforce ח־ז־ק from Journey 2 lesson 56. Introduce א־מ־צ (to be brave). This imperative pair will repeat three times in Joshua 1.
Root(s) introduced: א־מ־צ (to be brave).
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Memorization Reward, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recites chazak ve-ematz from memory.

Lesson 2. Turn Not from It to Right or Left
Verse: Joshua 1:7
אַל־תָּסוּר מִמֶּנּוּ יָמִין וּשְׂמֹאול
al-tasur mimenu yamin u-smol
Gloss: Turn not from it to the right hand or to the left.
Teaching focus: Reinforce ס־ו־ר from Journey 2 lesson 55. Introduce יָמִין (right hand) and שְׂמֹאל (left hand).
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: יָמִין (right), שְׂמֹאל (left).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Directional Sort, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child names right and left in Hebrew.

Lesson 3. Meditate on the Torah Day and Night

Lesson 4. The People Passed Over
Verse: Joshua 3:17
וְכָל־יִשְׂרָאֵל עֹבְרִים בֶּחָרֶבָה עַד אֲשֶׁר־תַּמּוּ כָּל־הַגּוֹי לַעֲבֹר אֶת־הַיַּרְדֵּן
ve-chol-Yisrael ovrim be-charava ad asher-tamu kol-ha-goi la'avor et-ha-Yarden
Gloss: And all Israel passed over on dry ground, until all the nation had finished crossing the Jordan.
Teaching focus: Reinforce ע־ב־ר (to pass over, from Journey 3 lesson 53). Introduce ת־מ־מ (to be complete) and חָרָבָה (dry ground). Strong echo of Exodus 14.
Root(s) introduced: ת־מ־מ (to be complete, finish).
New vocabulary: חָרָבָה (dry ground), יַרְדֵּן (Jordan).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Intertextual Echo Highlight (Exodus 14 parallel), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child names the Red Sea parallel.

Lesson 5. Twelve Stones for a Memorial

Lesson 6. Take Off Your Sandals

שַׁל־נַעַלְךָ מֵעַל רַגְלֶךָ כִּי הַמָּקוֹם אֲשֶׁר אַתָּה עֹמֵד עָלָיו קֹדֶשׁ הוּא

Lesson 7. The Walls Fell Down
Verse: Joshua 6:20
וַיָּרֵעוּ הָעָם וַיִּתְקְעוּ בַּשֹּׁפָרוֹת וַתִּפֹּל הַחוֹמָה תַּחְתֶּיהָ
va-yare'u ha-am va-yitke'u ba-shofarot va-tipol ha-choma tachteha
Gloss: And the people shouted and blew the trumpets, and the wall fell down.
Teaching focus: Introduce ר־ו־ע (to shout) in Hiphil. Introduce ת־ק־ע (to blow) in Qal. Introduce שׁוֹפָר (ram's horn) and חוֹמָה (wall).
Root(s) introduced: ר־ו־ע (to shout), ת־ק־ע (to blow, blast).
New vocabulary: שׁוֹפָר (shofar, ram's horn), חוֹמָה (wall).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 20 aloud.

Lesson 8. Choose You This Day
Verse: Joshua 24:15
בַּחֲרוּ לָכֶם הַיּוֹם אֶת־מִי תַעֲבֹדוּן
bacharu lachem ha-yom et-mi ta'avodun
Gloss: Choose this day whom you will serve.
Teaching focus: Introduce ב־ח־ר (to choose). Reinforce ע־ב־ד. The paragogic nun on ta'avodun (poetic / emphatic form). Joshua's farewell covenant-renewal.
Root(s) introduced: ב־ח־ר (to choose).
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Memorization Reward, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recites verse 15.

Gloss: And they forsook the LORD, God of their fathers.
Teaching focus: Reinforce ע־ז־ב from Journey 4 lesson 11. The Judges cycle opens.
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 12.

Lesson 10. The LORD Raised Up Judges
Verse: Judges 2:16
וַיָּקֶם יְהוָה שֹׁפְטִים וַיּוֹשִׁיעוּם מִיַּד שֹׁסֵיהֶם
va-yakem YHVH shoftim va-yoshi'um mi-yad shoseihem
Gloss: And the LORD raised up judges, and they saved them from the hand of their plunderers.
Teaching focus: Introduce the Hiphil va-yakem from k-v-m. Introduce שׁ־פ־ט (to judge) as Qal active participle. Reinforce y-sh-a in Hiphil (yoshi'um, they delivered them).
Root(s) introduced: ש־פ־ט (to judge).
New vocabulary: שׁוֹפֵט (judge).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Binyan Builder, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recognizes shofet and connects it to mishpat (judgment).

Lesson 11. Deborah the Prophetess
Verse: Judges 4:4
וּדְבוֹרָה אִשָּׁה נְבִיאָה הִיא שֹׁפְטָה אֶת־יִשְׂרָאֵל בָּעֵת הַהִיא
u-Dvora isha nevi'a hi shofta et-Yisrael ba-et ha-hi
Gloss: And Deborah, a prophetess, was judging Israel at that time.
Teaching focus: Introduce נְבִיאָה (prophetess, feminine of navi). Introduce the Qal active participle feminine singular shofta.
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: נְבִיאָה (prophetess), נָבִיא (prophet).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Gender Sort (masculine vs feminine participle), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child forms feminine participle.

Lesson 12. Awake, Awake, Deborah
Verse: Judges 5:12
עוּרִי עוּרִי דְּבוֹרָה עוּרִי עוּרִי דַּבְּרִי־שִׁיר
uri uri Dvora uri uri dabri-shir
Gloss: Awake, awake, Deborah, awake, awake, utter a song.
Teaching focus: Introduce ע־ו־ר (to awake) in Qal feminine imperative. Reinforce ד־ב־ר in Piel feminine imperative. The song of Deborah, a very ancient piece of Hebrew poetry.
Root(s) introduced: ע־ו־ר (to awake).
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Feminine Imperative Chain, Memorization Reward.
Exit check: Child recites the awakening refrain.

וַיִּבֶן שָׁם גִּדְעוֹן מִזְבֵּחַ לַיהוָה וַיִּקְרָא־לוֹ יְהוָה שָׁלוֹם

Unit 3. The Rise of Samuel (Lessons 17 to 24)
Source text: 1 Samuel 1 through 3, 15 through 17 (selected verses). Hannah's prayer, Samuel's call, the request for a king, Saul rejected, David anointed, David and Goliath. Hannah's prayer (1 Samuel 2) is a major poetic text introduced gently.
Lesson 17. Hannah Prayed
Verse: 1 Samuel 1:10
וְהִיא מָרַת נָפֶשׁ וַתִּתְפַּלֵּל עַל־יְהוָה וּבָכֹה תִבְכֶּה
ve-hi marat nafesh va-titpalel al-YHVH u-vacho tivkeh
Gloss: And she was bitter of soul and prayed to the LORD and wept bitterly.
Teaching focus: Reinforce Hithpael of p-l-l from Journey 4 lesson 5. Introduce מַר (bitter). Reinforce the emphatic infinitive absolute plus imperfect (bacho tivkeh, surely she wept).
Root(s) introduced: מ־ר־ר (to be bitter).
New vocabulary: מַר (bitter).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 10.

Lesson 18. My Heart Rejoices in the LORD

עָלַץ לִבִּי בַּיהוָה רָמָה קַרְנִי בַּיהוָה

Gloss: My heart rejoices in the LORD, my horn is exalted in the LORD.
Teaching focus: Introduce ע־ל־צ (to rejoice). Introduce the horn-of-strength metaphor (karni, my horn).
Root(s) introduced: ע־ל־צ (to rejoice).
New vocabulary: קֶרֶן (horn).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort (joy-verbs now four: s-m-ch, g-y-l, r-n-n, a-l-tz), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child names four joy-verbs.

Unit 4. David and the Psalms (Lessons 25 to 32)
Source text: selected verses from 2 Samuel 7 (the Davidic covenant), Psalm 23, Psalm 51. The Davidic covenant passage is theologically weighty. Psalm 23 is the most beloved psalm in the tradition; Psalm 51 introduces full Hebrew lament-structure.
Lesson 25. The LORD Is My Shepherd
Verse: Psalm 23:1
יְהוָה רֹעִי לֹא אֶחְסָר
YHVH ro'i lo echsar
Gloss: The LORD is my shepherd, I shall not lack.
Teaching focus: Introduce ר־ע־ה (to shepherd). Introduce ח־ס־ר (to lack). The opening of Psalm 23, which the child now has the Hebrew to begin reading in its original language.
Root(s) introduced: ר־ע־ה (to shepherd), ח־ס־ר (to lack).
New vocabulary: רֹעֶה (shepherd).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Memorization Reward (the most famous Hebrew psalm verse), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recites Psalm 23:1 from memory.

Lesson 26. He Leads Me Beside Still Waters
Verse: Psalm 23:2
בִּנְאוֹת דֶּשֶׁא יַרְבִּיצֵנִי עַל־מֵי מְנֻחוֹת יְנַהֲלֵנִי
bi-n'ot deshe yarbitzeni al-mei menuchot yenahaleni
Gloss: In pastures of grass he makes me lie down, beside waters of rest he leads me.
Teaching focus: Introduce the Hiphil yarbitzeni (he makes me lie down, from r-v-tz). Introduce the Piel yenahaleni (he leads me, from n-h-l). Introduce מְנוּחָה (rest).
Root(s) introduced: ר־ב־צ (to lie down), נ־ה־ל (to lead gently), נ־ו־ח (rest).
New vocabulary: דֶּשֶׁא (grass), מְנוּחָה (rest).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Binyan Builder, Memorization Reward.
Exit check: Child recites Psalm 23:2.

Mini-games used: Chant Along, Memorization Reward, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recites Psalm 23:4.
