A Reading Course in Biblical and Restoration Hebrew for Children

Design Draft v1.0
 
Journey 3 Overview
Source text: Exodus 1 through 20 in depth, Exodus 32 through 34, and selected passages from Numbers 6 and 21. Journey 3 is the theological core of the Tanakh reading track. It teaches the child the full covenant vocabulary (berit, chesed, mitzvah, torah, pesach, geulah) in its native habitat before any restoration scripture is encountered in Journey 4. The child reads the Decalogue in full and the Shema as Journey exit.
Journey 3 is 70 lessons across eight units. By this Journey, the child reads Hebrew narrative prose fluently, parses most verb forms without help, and begins encountering poetic parallelism in Exodus 15 (the Song of the Sea) as preparation for Journey 4's Nephi's psalm.
Niqqud Fading Schedule
By Journey 3, niqqud fading has advanced. The child reads high-frequency words (common verbs, prepositions, conjunctions, proper names of God) without pointing on most occurrences. New vocabulary and less common words retain full pointing. By the end of Journey 3, roughly 40 percent of the text the child sees is unpointed.
 
Unit 1. The Oppression (Lessons 1 to 8)
Source text: Exodus 1 through 2. Israel in Egypt, the new Pharaoh who did not know Joseph, the midwives, the birth of Moses, Moses in Midian.
Lesson 1. A New King Arose
Verse: Exodus 1:8
וַיָּקָם מֶלֶךְ־חָדָשׁ עַל־מִצְרָיִם אֲשֶׁר לֹא־יָדַע אֶת־יוֹסֵף
va-yakom melech-chadash al-Mitzrayim asher lo-yada et-Yosef
Gloss: And a new king arose over Egypt who did not know Joseph.
Teaching focus: Introduce מֶלֶךְ (king), a high-frequency word. Introduce חָדָשׁ (new). Reinforce ק־ו־מ (to rise) and י־ד־ע (to know).
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce ק־ו־מ.
New vocabulary: מֶלֶךְ (king), חָדָשׁ (new).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 8 aloud with minimal support.

Lesson 2. The Midwives Feared God
Verse: Exodus 1:17
וַתִּירֶאןָ הַמְיַלְּדֹת אֶת־הָאֱלֹהִים וְלֹא עָשׂוּ כַּאֲשֶׁר דִּבֶּר אֲלֵיהֶן מֶלֶךְ מִצְרָיִם
va-tirena ha-meyaldot et-ha-Elohim ve-lo asu ka-asher diber aleihen melech Mitzrayim
Gloss: And the midwives feared God and did not do as the king of Egypt had spoken to them.
Teaching focus: Introduce the 3fp imperfect va-tirena (they feared, feminine plural). Introduce the Piel participle feminine plural מְיַלְּדֹת (midwives, ones causing to bear). A rare and valuable 3fp exposure.
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce י־ר־א, י־ל־ד.
New vocabulary: מְיַלֶּדֶת (midwife).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Gender and Number Sort, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child identifies a 3fp verb form.

Lesson 3. She Hid Him Three Months
Verse: Exodus 2:2
וַתַּהַר הָאִשָּׁה וַתֵּלֶד בֵּן וַתֵּרֶא אֹתוֹ כִּי־טוֹב הוּא וַתִּצְפְּנֵהוּ שְׁלֹשָׁה יְרָחִים
va-tahar ha-isha va-teled ben va-tere oto ki-tov hu va-titzpenehu shlosha yerachim
Gloss: And the woman conceived and bore a son, and saw him that he was good, and hid him three months.
Teaching focus: Introduce צ־פ־נ (to hide). Introduce יֶרַח (month) as plural. Echo of Genesis 1 "saw it was good" language.
Root(s) introduced: צ־פ־נ (to hide).
New vocabulary: יֶרַח (month).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Intertextual Echo Highlight (the ki-tov formula from Genesis 1).
Exit check: Child notices the echo of Genesis 1 in the description of Moses.

Lesson 4. From the Water I Drew Him
Verse: Exodus 2:10
וַתִּקְרָא שְׁמוֹ מֹשֶׁה וַתֹּאמֶר כִּי מִן־הַמַּיִם מְשִׁיתִהוּ
va-tikra shmo Moshe va-tomer ki min-ha-mayim meshitihu
Gloss: And she called his name Moses and said, For out of the water I drew him.
Teaching focus: Introduce the name pun: Moshe (drawn out) from מ־שׁ־ה (to draw out). The naming formula parallels Isaac and Abraham.
Root(s) introduced: מ־שׁ־ה (to draw out).
New vocabulary: מֹשֶׁה (Moses).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Name Meaning Highlight, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child explains the pun between Moshe and the verb meaning "to draw out."

Lesson 5. He Went Out to His Brethren
Verse: Exodus 2:11
וַיְהִי בַּיָּמִים הָהֵם וַיִּגְדַּל מֹשֶׁה וַיֵּצֵא אֶל־אֶחָיו וַיַּרְא בְּסִבְלֹתָם
va-yehi ba-yamim ha-hem va-yigdal Moshe va-yetze el-echav va-yar be-sivlotam
Gloss: And it came to pass in those days that Moses grew up and went out to his brethren and saw their burdens.
Teaching focus: Introduce the narrative frame va-yehi ba-yamim ha-hem (and it came to pass in those days). Reinforce ג־ד־ל, י־צ־א, ר־א־ה.
Root(s) introduced: ס־ב־ל (to bear a burden).
New vocabulary: סִבְלָה (burden), אַחִים (brethren, plural of אָח).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Narrative Frame Highlight, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recognizes va-yehi as a narrative opener.

Lesson 6. He Fled to Midian
Verse: Exodus 2:15
וַיִּבְרַח מֹשֶׁה מִפְּנֵי פַרְעֹה וַיֵּשֶׁב בְּאֶרֶץ־מִדְיָן
va-yivrach Moshe mi-pnei Par'oh va-yeshev be-eretz-Midyan
Gloss: And Moses fled from before Pharaoh and dwelt in the land of Midian.
Teaching focus: Introduce ב־ר־ח (to flee). Introduce the idiom מִפְּנֵי (from before).
Root(s) introduced: ב־ר־ח (to flee).
New vocabulary: מִדְיָן (Midian).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Preposition Builder (from-the-face-of).
Exit check: Child reads verse 15 with minimal support.

Lesson 7. God Heard Their Groaning
Verse: Exodus 2:24
וַיִּשְׁמַע אֱלֹהִים אֶת־נַאֲקָתָם וַיִּזְכֹּר אֱלֹהִים אֶת־בְּרִיתוֹ אֶת־אַבְרָהָם אֶת־יִצְחָק וְאֶת־יַעֲקֹב
va-yishma Elohim et-na'akatam va-yizkor Elohim et-berito et-Avraham et-Yitzchak ve-et-Ya'akov
Gloss: And God heard their groaning, and God remembered his covenant with Abraham, with Isaac, and with Jacob.
Teaching focus: Introduce ש־מ־ע (to hear) and ז־כ־ר (to remember). Both are massively high-frequency and covenantally weighty. The covenant-remembering formula appears explicitly.
Root(s) introduced: ש־מ־ע (to hear), ז־כ־ר (to remember).
New vocabulary: נַאֲקָה (groaning).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Covenant Vocabulary Review (berit highlighted).
Exit check: Child recites the covenant-remembering formula.

Lesson 8. God Knew
Verse: Exodus 2:25
וַיַּרְא אֱלֹהִים אֶת־בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל וַיֵּדַע אֱלֹהִים
va-yar Elohim et-bnei Yisrael va-yeda Elohim
Gloss: And God saw the children of Israel, and God knew.
Teaching focus: Short, weighty verse. Reinforce ר־א־ה and י־ד־ע in 3ms vav-consecutive. Memorization target.
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Memorization Reward, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recites verse 25 from memory.

 
Unit 2. The Burning Bush (Lessons 9 to 16)
Source text: Exodus 3 through 4. The call of Moses, the divine name revealed, the signs, the return to Egypt. Exodus 3:14 (I AM THAT I AM) is the central verse and a critical moment in the child's Hebrew education.
Lesson 9. The Bush Burned with Fire
Verse: Exodus 3:2
וְהִנֵּה הַסְּנֶה בֹּעֵר בָּאֵשׁ וְהַסְּנֶה אֵינֶנּוּ אֻכָּל
ve-hineh ha-sneh bo'er ba-esh ve-ha-sneh einenu ukkal
Gloss: And behold, the bush burned with fire, and the bush was not consumed.
Teaching focus: Introduce ב־ע־ר (to burn). Introduce the negative existence particle אֵין with suffix (einenu, it is not). Introduce the Pual participle אֻכָּל (consumed, passive).
Root(s) introduced: ב־ע־ר (to burn).
New vocabulary: סְנֶה (bush), אֵשׁ (fire).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Existence Particle Match (yesh / ein), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child negates a yesh-statement with ein-plus-suffix.

Lesson 10. Put Off Thy Shoes
Verse: Exodus 3:5
שַׁל־נְעָלֶיךָ מֵעַל רַגְלֶיךָ כִּי הַמָּקוֹם אֲשֶׁר אַתָּה עוֹמֵד עָלָיו אַדְמַת־קֹדֶשׁ הוּא
shal-ne'alecha me-al raglecha ki ha-makom asher ata omed alav admat-kodesh hu
Gloss: Put off your shoes from your feet, for the place on which you are standing is holy ground.
Teaching focus: Introduce נ־שׁ־ל (to draw off). Introduce the imperative plus 2ms suffix. Introduce the construct אַדְמַת־קֹדֶשׁ (ground of holiness). Reinforce ק־ד־שׁ as a weighty root.
Root(s) introduced: נ־שׁ־ל (to draw off, remove), ע־מ־ד (to stand).
New vocabulary: נַעַל (shoe, sandal), קֹדֶשׁ (holiness).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Construct Chain Builder, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recognizes the construct admat-kodesh and translates it.

Lesson 11. I Have Surely Seen
Verse: Exodus 3:7
רָאֹה רָאִיתִי אֶת־עֳנִי עַמִּי אֲשֶׁר בְּמִצְרָיִם
ra'oh ra'iti et-oni ami asher be-Mitzrayim
Gloss: I have surely seen the affliction of my people who are in Egypt.
Teaching focus: Introduce the emphatic infinitive absolute plus perfect (ra'oh ra'iti, surely I have seen). Introduce עֹנִי (affliction) and עַם (people, my people).
Root(s) introduced: ע־נ־ה (to afflict, answer — one of the polysemic roots).
New vocabulary: עֳנִי (affliction), עַם (people).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Emphatic Construction Highlight, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child forms an inf absolute plus perfect emphatic construction from a familiar root.

Lesson 12. I Will Be with Thee
Verse: Exodus 3:12
וַיֹּאמֶר כִּי־אֶהְיֶה עִמָּךְ
va-yomer ki-eheyeh imach
Gloss: And he said, I will be with you.
Teaching focus: Short, weighty verse. Introduce the 1cs imperfect of ה־י־ה (eheyeh, I will be). Covenant-accompaniment formula.
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Memorization Reward, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recites verse 12 from memory in Hebrew.

Lesson 13. I AM THAT I AM
Verse: Exodus 3:14
וַיֹּאמֶר אֱלֹהִים אֶל־מֹשֶׁה אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה
va-yomer Elohim el-Moshe eheyeh asher eheyeh
Gloss: And God said to Moses, I AM THAT I AM.
Teaching focus: The central divine self-identification. Explain the root connection: the Tetragrammaton יְהוָה is etymologically linked to this verb of being. For LDS families, note that this name is identified with Jehovah / premortal Christ. Parent companion material provides depth.
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce ה־י־ה as the root of the divine name.
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Divine Name Highlight (the child sees YHVH and eheyeh glow together), Memorization Reward.
Exit check: Child recites verse 14 from memory and explains the connection between eheyeh and YHVH.

Lesson 14. The God of Your Fathers
Verse: Exodus 3:15
יְהוָה אֱלֹהֵי אֲבֹתֵיכֶם אֱלֹהֵי אַבְרָהָם אֱלֹהֵי יִצְחָק וֵאלֹהֵי יַעֲקֹב שְׁלָחַנִי אֲלֵיכֶם זֶה־שְּׁמִי לְעֹלָם
YHVH Elohei avotechem Elohei Avraham Elohei Yitzchak v-Elohei Ya'akov shelachani aleichem zeh-shmi le-olam
Gloss: The LORD, God of your fathers, God of Abraham, God of Isaac, and God of Jacob, has sent me to you. This is my name forever.
Teaching focus: Introduce אָב in construct plural (avot, avotechem). Introduce עוֹלָם (forever, age, world). A weighty covenant-name verse.
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce ש־ל־ח with object suffix.
New vocabulary: עוֹלָם (forever, eternity).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Construct Chain Builder, Memorization Reward, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recites the Elohei Avraham / Elohei Yitzchak / Elohei Ya'akov formula.

Lesson 15. What Is That in Thy Hand
Verse: Exodus 4:2
וַיֹּאמֶר אֵלָיו יְהוָה מַה־זֶּה בְיָדֶךָ וַיֹּאמֶר מַטֶּה
va-yomer elav YHVH ma-zeh ve-yadecha va-yomer mateh
Gloss: And the LORD said to him, What is that in your hand? And he said, A rod.
Teaching focus: Introduce the interrogative מַה (what). A short, memorable dialogue.
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: מַטֶּה (rod, staff).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Question Word Tap, Dialogue Builder, Memorization Reward.
Exit check: Child asks and answers a simple mah-question in Hebrew.

Lesson 16. I Am Slow of Speech
Verse: Exodus 4:10
לֹא אִישׁ דְּבָרִים אָנֹכִי כִּי כְבַד־פֶּה וּכְבַד לָשׁוֹן אָנֹכִי
lo ish devarim anochi ki chvad-peh u-chvad lashon anochi
Gloss: I am not a man of words, for I am heavy of mouth and heavy of tongue.
Teaching focus: Introduce the construct chvad-peh (heavy of mouth). Introduce לָשׁוֹן (tongue). Reinforce the emphatic pronoun anochi.
Root(s) introduced: כ־ב־ד (to be heavy).
New vocabulary: פֶּה (mouth, reinforced), לָשׁוֹן (tongue).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Construct Chain Builder, Shoresh Sort, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 10 aloud with minimal support.

 
Unit 3. Let My People Go (Lessons 17 to 24)
Source text: Exodus 5, 7 through 11 (selected verses). The confrontations with Pharaoh, the plagues, the hardening of Pharaoh's heart. Exodus 9 and 10 are sampled; the full plague sequence is summarized in the parent companion material.
Lesson 17. Thus Says the LORD
Verse: Exodus 5:1
כֹּה אָמַר יְהוָה אֱלֹהֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל שַׁלַּח אֶת־עַמִּי
koh amar YHVH Elohei Yisrael shalach et-ami
Gloss: Thus says the LORD, God of Israel, Let my people go.
Teaching focus: Introduce the prophetic messenger formula koh amar YHVH. Introduce the Piel imperative שַׁלַּח (let go, send away).
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce ש־ל־ח in Piel.
New vocabulary: כֹּה (thus).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Prophetic Formula Highlight, Binyan Builder (Qal vs Piel of sh-l-ch), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recognizes koh amar YHVH as the prophetic messenger formula.

Lesson 18. Who Is the LORD
Verse: Exodus 5:2
מִי יְהוָה אֲשֶׁר אֶשְׁמַע בְּקֹלוֹ
mi YHVH asher eshma be-kolo
Gloss: Who is the LORD that I should listen to his voice?
Teaching focus: Introduce the interrogative מִי (who). Introduce the idiom שמע בקול (listen to the voice of, i.e. obey).
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Question Word Tap, Idiom Highlight, Memorization Reward.
Exit check: Child asks a mi-question in Hebrew.

Lesson 19. I Will Harden Pharaoh's Heart
Verse: Exodus 7:3
וַאֲנִי אַקְשֶׁה אֶת־לֵב פַּרְעֹה
va-ani akshe et-lev Par'oh
Gloss: And I will harden Pharaoh's heart.
Teaching focus: Introduce the Hiphil akshe (I will harden) from ק־שׁ־ה. Introduce לֵב (heart), a central biblical-Hebrew word.
Root(s) introduced: ק־שׁ־ה (to be hard).
New vocabulary: לֵב (heart).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Binyan Builder (Qal vs Hiphil), Shoresh Sort, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child forms Hiphil of ק־שׁ־ה.

Lesson 20. The Rod Became a Serpent
Verse: Exodus 7:10
וַיַּשְׁלֵךְ אַהֲרֹן אֶת־מַטֵּהוּ לִפְנֵי פַרְעֹה וְלִפְנֵי עֲבָדָיו וַיְהִי לְתַנִּין
va-yashlech Aharon et-matehu lifnei Par'oh ve-lifnei avadav va-yehi le-tanin

Teaching focus: Introduce the Hiphil va-yashlech (he cast). Introduce תַּנִּין (sea-monster, serpent).
Root(s) introduced: ש־ל־כ (to cast, throw).
New vocabulary: תַּנִּין (serpent, sea-monster), עֶבֶד (servant).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Binyan Builder, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child forms Hiphil of ש־ל־כ.

Lesson 21. The Waters Became Blood
Verse: Exodus 7:20
וַיֵּהָפְכוּ כָּל־הַמַּיִם אֲשֶׁר בַּיְאֹר לְדָם
va-yehafchu kol-ha-mayim asher ba-ye'or le-dam
Gloss: And all the waters which were in the river were turned to blood.
Teaching focus: Introduce ה־פ־כ (to turn, overturn) in Niphal. Introduce דָּם (blood).

Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Binyan Builder, Verse Builder.

Lesson 22. Pharaoh's Heart Was Hard
Verse: Exodus 7:13
וַיֶּחֱזַק לֵב פַּרְעֹה וְלֹא שָׁמַע אֲלֵהֶם
va-yechezak lev Par'oh ve-lo shama alehem
Gloss: And Pharaoh's heart was strong, and he did not listen to them.
Teaching focus: Reinforce ח־ז־ק. Contrast with Journey 2 lesson 56 (Israel strengthened himself in Hithpael) vs Qal here (was strong).

Mini-games used: Chant Along, Binyan Builder (Qal vs Hithpael), Verse Builder.

Lesson 23. I Know the LORD
Verse: Exodus 8:6
לְמַעַן תֵּדַע כִּי־אֵין כַּיהוָה אֱלֹהֵינוּ
lema'an teda ki-ein ka-YHVH Eloheinu
Gloss: That you may know that there is none like the LORD our God.
Teaching focus: Introduce the purpose clause לְמַעַן (in order that). Introduce the comparative כְּ plus divine name. Introduce Eloheinu (our God) with 1cp suffix.
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: לְמַעַן (in order that).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Purpose Clause Builder, Verse Builder.

Lesson 24. Not a Dog Shall Move His Tongue
Verse: Exodus 11:7
וּלְכֹל בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל לֹא יֶחֱרַץ־כֶּלֶב לְשֹׁנוֹ

Gloss: But against all the children of Israel not a dog shall move his tongue.
Teaching focus: Introduce ח־ר־צ (to move, sharpen). Introduce כֶּלֶב (dog). A vivid idiom of absolute quiet.
Root(s) introduced: ח־ר־צ.
New vocabulary: כֶּלֶב (dog).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Idiom Highlight, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 7 aloud.

 
Unit 4. The Passover (Lessons 25 to 32)
Source text: Exodus 12. The Passover instructions and the exodus itself. This unit anchors the pesach / geulah vocabulary that will be foundational for Journey 4 (Lehi's family departure parallels the exodus) and beyond.
Lesson 25. This Month Shall Be to You
Verse: Exodus 12:2
הַחֹדֶשׁ הַזֶּה לָכֶם רֹאשׁ חֳדָשִׁים רִאשׁוֹן הוּא לָכֶם לְחָדְשֵׁי הַשָּׁנָה
ha-chodesh ha-zeh lachem rosh chodashim rishon hu lachem le-chodshei ha-shana
Gloss: This month shall be to you the head of months, the first it shall be to you of the months of the year.
Teaching focus: Introduce חֹדֶשׁ (new moon, month). Introduce the calendar vocabulary.
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce ח־ד־שׁ (to be new).
New vocabulary: חֹדֶשׁ (month), שָׁנָה (year, reinforced).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Construct Chain Builder, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 2 aloud and identifies rosh chodashim.

Appendix C. Journey 3 Exit to Journey 4
Journey 3 completion is the largest milestone of the course so far. The child now reads Biblical Hebrew narrative and simple poetry with reasonable fluency. Their active vocabulary crosses 300 words and over 100 roots. The scribe avatar earns a priestly sash. The menorah lights three times. Journey 4 (Lehi's Family) unlocks.
This is the pivot moment of the whole course. The child's next lesson opens Sefer Mormon for the first time, and they will recognize that the Hebrew they are reading sounds just like the Hebrew of Genesis 12. That recognition is the convergent validation framework delivered experientially, without a single word of lecture. The parent companion material for Journey 4 lesson 1 is extensive; it explains to the parent exactly what the child is seeing and why it matters.
