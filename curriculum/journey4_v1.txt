Journey 4 Overview
Source text: 1 Nephi 1 through 18 from Chris's Classical Biblical Hebrew translation (Sefer Mormon). Journey 4 is the pivot Journey of the course. For the first time the child opens restoration scripture in Hebrew, and they immediately recognize that the Hebrew sounds just like the Hebrew of Genesis 12. The covenant vocabulary they anchored in Journey 3 (chesed, berit, mitzvah, torah, yeshua) begins to reappear, and the child experiences convergent validation as recognition rather than argument.
Journey 4 is 60 lessons across seven units. The child reads Nephi's opening autobiographical formula, the Tree of Life vision (which echoes Genesis 2 and 3), Nephi's psalm (which draws on the Psalter and introduces Hithpael in its most expressive form), and the sea voyage to the promised land (which echoes Exodus 14). Each echo is felt, not explained.
Alternate Path for Non-LDS Users
For subscribers outside the LDS tradition, Journey 4 offers an alternate path covering Isaiah 40 through 55 (Second Isaiah) with the same grammar targets and same pedagogical arc. The Aleph Trail app detects the user's chosen path during onboarding and serves the appropriate lesson sequence. Both paths converge at Journey 5. The parent companion material for the LDS path includes the non-LDS-path verses as optional supplementary reading.
Pedagogical Note for Parents
Journey 4 opens Sefer Mormon, Chris's Classical Biblical Hebrew translation of the Book of Mormon. The translation follows strict pre-exilic Mikraic register, the same register the child has been reading since Journey 1. If the parent compares Genesis 12:1 with 1 Nephi 2:2, they will see the same grammatical patterns and the same covenant vocabulary. This is not a coincidence. Chris has designed his translation so that the Hebrew of 1 Nephi testifies of the same God in the same language as the Hebrew of Genesis. This is convergent validation, delivered at the level of language rather than argument.
 
Unit 1. Nephi's Opening (Lessons 1 to 8)
Source text: 1 Nephi 1 in full. Nephi's autobiographical formula, Lehi's prayer, the pillar of fire, the book handed from the heavens. The opening chapter establishes the whole literary framework of the Book of Mormon and does so with Hebrew that feels like a direct continuation of the patriarchal narratives.
Lesson 1. I, Nephi, Having Been Born
Verse: 1 Nephi 1:1
אֲנִי נְפִי נוֹלַדְתִּי לְהוֹרִים טוֹבִים עַל־כֵּן לֻמַּדְתִּי בְּכָל־תּוֹרַת אָבִי
ani Nefi noladti le-horim tovim al-ken lumadti be-chol-torat avi
Gloss: I, Nephi, having been born to good parents, therefore was I taught in all the learning of my father.
Teaching focus: Introduce the Niphal noladti (I was born). Introduce לִמֵּד in Pual lumadti (I was taught). Reinforce תּוֹרָה (Torah, learning, instruction) as the noun of י־ר־ה. The child immediately recognizes תּוֹרָה from Journey 3 lesson 40. This recognition is the first taste of convergent validation.
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce י־ל־ד (Niphal), ל־מ־ד (Pual), י־ר־ה (noun).
New vocabulary: הוֹרִים (parents), תּוֹרָה (Torah, learning).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Convergent Validation Highlight (the word torah glows to mark recognition across Standard Works), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recognizes torah from Journey 3 and recites the opening verse.

Lesson 2. Many Afflictions in My Days
Verse: 1 Nephi 1:1b
וַאֲנִי רָאִיתִי צָרוֹת רַבּוֹת בְּיָמַי
va-ani ra'iti tzarot rabot be-yamai
Gloss: And I, having seen many afflictions in my days.
Teaching focus: Introduce צָרָה (affliction, distress) in feminine plural. Reinforce ר־א־ה in 1cs perfect with emphatic pronoun.
Root(s) introduced: צ־ר־ר (to be narrow, to distress).
New vocabulary: צָרָה (affliction).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child reads verse 1b and identifies the emphatic va-ani construction.

Lesson 3. The Goodness of God
Verse: 1 Nephi 1:1c
וּלְמַדְתִּי גַּם־כֵּן אֶת־טוֹב יְהוָה בְּכָל־יָמַי
u-lamadti gam-ken et-tov YHVH be-chol-yamai
Gloss: And I have learned also the goodness of the LORD in all my days.
Teaching focus: Introduce ל־מ־ד in Qal 1cs perfect. Reinforce the construct tov YHVH (goodness of the LORD).
Root(s) introduced: ל־מ־ד (to learn).
New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Binyan Builder (Qal vs Pual of l-m-d from lesson 1), Construct Chain Builder, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child distinguishes lamad (he learned) from lumad (he was taught).

Lesson 4. A Record of My Proceedings
Verse: 1 Nephi 1:1d
עַל־כֵּן אֶעֱשֶׂה שֵׁפֶר שֶׁל־מַעֲשַׂי בְּיָמַי
al-ken e'eseh sefer shel-ma'asai be-yamai
Gloss: Therefore I make a record of my proceedings in my days.
Teaching focus: Introduce מַעֲשֶׂה (deed, act) in plural with 1cs suffix. Introduce סֵפֶר (book, scroll) — the child connects this to the root ס־פ־ר learned in Journey 2 lesson 11. Another moment of recognition.
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce ס־פ־ר (sefer as noun).
New vocabulary: סֵפֶר (book, scroll), מַעֲשֶׂה (deed, work).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Shoresh Sort, Noun Family Highlight (sofer scribe, sefer book, lispor to count), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child names three nouns / verbs from the s-p-r family.

Lesson 5. Lehi Prayed to the LORD
Verse: 1 Nephi 1:5
עַל־כֵּן לֵחִי אָבִי הָלַךְ וְהִתְפַּלֵּל אֶל־יְהוָה בְּכָל־לֵב בְּעַד עַמּוֹ
al-ken Lechi avi halach ve-hitpalel el-YHVH be-chol-lev be'ad amo
Gloss: Therefore Lehi my father went and prayed to the LORD with all his heart on behalf of his people.
Teaching focus: Introduce the Hithpael hitpalel (he prayed). Introduce פ־ל־ל (to pray, intercede, judge). Introduce the preposition בְּעַד (on behalf of).
Root(s) introduced: פ־ל־ל (to pray, intercede).
New vocabulary: לֵחִי (Lehi).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Binyan Builder (Hithpael prominent), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child forms Hithpael of p-l-l and recognizes the prayer root.

Lesson 6. A Pillar of Fire Came

Gloss: And there came a pillar of fire and stood before him upon a rock.
Teaching focus: Introduce עַמּוּד (pillar). Introduce צוּר (rock, stronghold). The child recognizes amud-esh from Exodus (pillar of fire in the wilderness). Strong intertextual echo.
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce ע־מ־ד (to stand).
New vocabulary: עַמּוּד (pillar), צוּר (rock).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Intertextual Echo Highlight (pillar of fire echoes Exodus), Construct Chain Builder, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child names the Exodus parallel (pillar of fire leading Israel in the wilderness).

Unit 2. The Call and the Departure (Lessons 9 to 16)
Source text: 1 Nephi 2. Lehi's command to depart into the wilderness, the family's flight from Jerusalem, Nephi's prayer of faith. This chapter intentionally echoes Abraham's lech-lecha from Genesis 12:1, which the child read in Journey 2 lesson 1.
Lesson 9. Take Thy Family and Depart
Verse: 1 Nephi 2:2
וַיְצַו אֹתוֹ יְהוָה לַאמֹר קַח־נָא אֶת־מִשְׁפַּחְתְּךָ וְצֵא לַמִּדְבָּר
va-yetzav oto YHVH lemor kach-na et-mishpachtecha ve-tze la-midbar
Gloss: And the LORD commanded him, saying, Take now your family and go forth into the wilderness.
Teaching focus: Introduce the Piel va-yetzav (he commanded, from ts-v-h, reinforced from Journey 3 lesson 64). Reinforce the imperative kach from Journey 2 lesson 20. Introduce מִדְבָּר (wilderness). Strong echo of Genesis 12:1 (lech-lecha).
Root(s) introduced: Reinforce צ־ו־ה, ל־ק־ח, י־צ־א.
New vocabulary: מִדְבָּר (wilderness), מִשְׁפָּחָה (family, reinforced).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Intertextual Echo Highlight (Abrahamic call parallel), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child identifies the Genesis 12:1 parallel without being told.

Lesson 10. He Was Obedient to the Word
Verse: 1 Nephi 2:3
וַיְהִי שֹׁמֵעַ בְּקוֹל יְהוָה עַל־כֵּן לֹא עָשָׂה דָבָר מִבַּד אֲשֶׁר צִוָּהוּ יְהוָה
va-yehi shomea be-kol YHVH al-ken lo asa davar mi-bad asher tzivahu YHVH
Gloss: And he was hearkening to the voice of the LORD, therefore he did not a thing apart from what the LORD commanded him.
Teaching focus: Reinforce the idiom shomea be-kol (hearkening to the voice, i.e. obeying). Reinforce the Piel perfect with 3ms suffix tzivahu.
Root(s) introduced: None new.
New vocabulary: מִבַּד (apart from, without).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Idiom Highlight (obedience idiom from Exodus), Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child recognizes the obedience idiom from Journey 3.

Lesson 11. He Left His House
Verse: 1 Nephi 2:4
וַיַּעֲזֹב אֶת־בֵּיתוֹ וְאֶת־נַחֲלָתוֹ וְאֶת־זְהָבוֹ וְאֶת־כַּסְפּוֹ

Teaching focus: Introduce ע־ז־ב (to leave, forsake). Introduce נַחֲלָה (inheritance), a weighty covenant term. Reinforce זָהָב (gold, from Journey 1) and כֶּסֶף (silver, from Journey 2).
Root(s) introduced: ע־ז־ב (to leave, forsake).
New vocabulary: נַחֲלָה (inheritance).
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Possessive Suffix Chain Builder, Verse Builder.
Exit check: Child forms a chain of possessive suffixes and recognizes nachala as a covenant term.

Unit 5. Nephi's Psalm (Lessons 35 to 42)
Source text: 2 Nephi 4:16-35 (selected verses). Nephi's psalm of lament, the first substantial poetic passage in restoration scripture. The Hebrew form is designed to sound like Psalm 22 and Psalm 51. This unit introduces the Hithpael in its most expressive emotional form and deepens the child's familiarity with parallelism.

New vocabulary: None new.
Mini-games used: Chant Along, Binyan Builder (Hithpael), Verse Builder.

Appendix B. The Go'el Root Introduction
Journey 4 introduces ג־א־ל (goel, to redeem as kinsman-redeemer) for the first time in the course. This root is the single most important piece of theological vocabulary Chris's Covenant King commentary on Mosiah will build on. Once introduced here in 2 Nephi 4:31, the root reappears in Ruth (Journey 7), throughout Isaiah (Journey 7), and most heavily in Mosiah (Journey 6).
The Aleph Trail app tracks this root as a high-frequency covenant term. Every time the child sees it, it receives the gentle gold-treatment highlight reserved for foundational covenant vocabulary (alongside berit, chesed, mitzvah, torah). By the end of the course, the child has encountered goel dozens of times and intuits its meaning without translation.
Appendix C. Convergent Validation Moments in Journey 4
Journey 4 is designed so that the child experiences the convergent validation framework as recognition, not argument. The explicit recognition moments in this Journey are:
•	Lesson 1: torah appears in 1 Nephi 1:1, recognized from Journey 3 lesson 40.
•	Lesson 4: sefer appears, recognized from Journey 2 lesson 11.
•	Lesson 9: the Genesis 12:1 lech-lecha pattern is echoed in 1 Nephi 2:2.
•	Lesson 19: mitzvah reappears from Journey 3 lesson 64.
•	Lesson 29: the tree whose fruit is desirable echoes Genesis 3:6.
•	Lesson 46: rachem echoes rachum from Exodus 34:6 (the thirteen attributes).
•	Lesson 53: Moses smiting the rock echoes Exodus 17.
•	Lesson 55: the earth obeying the divine word echoes Genesis 1.
These echoes are never explained in-lesson to the child. They are allowed to surface as recognition. The parent companion material names them explicitly so parents can reinforce the pattern if they choose.
Appendix D. Journey 4 Exit to Journey 5
On Journey 4 completion, the child's scribe avatar earns a prophetic robe. The menorah lights four times. Journey 5 (Land and Kings) unlocks, returning to Tanakh material with Joshua, Judges, Samuel, and Kings. The child now has the foundational covenant vocabulary of both Tanakh and Sefer Mormon anchored in active use.
